MLBD

In this research, we are dedicated to developing comprehensive skills in applying machine learning techniques to solve real-world problems. We tackle challenges across data handling—selecting and preparing appropriate datasets—and software development, focusing on constructing robust machine learning pipelines. Additionally, our research emphasizes optimizing the use of advanced computing resources like GPUs to enhance performance. By integrating these elements, we aim to advance practical applications and innovations in machine learning and big data across diverse sectors.


Risk Communcation & Decision-Making

Research on how complex risk information impacts decision-making processes in critical areas such as healthcare, finance, and emergency response. Our research aims to understand how different factors, including personal skills, abilities, and personality traits, influence the way individuals make informed decisions in the face of high-stakes situations and emerging threats. We develop, evaluate, and analyze risk communication strategies to assess their effect on decision-making behaviors, aiming to enhance effectiveness in real-world scenarios.


HATLab (Humans and Technology Research Lab)

The HATLab is a multidisciplinary space where students, faculty and researchers come together to investigate all aspects of humans' interactions with technology. In particular, we are interested in human-centered computing, human factors, human-computer interaction, health informatics, usable privacy and security, privacy-enhancing technologies, empirical methods and designing for special populations.


Summer REU for Learning & Big Data Creative Inquiry

Our research team worked on a paper after the conducted research on enhancing environment perception systems for autonomous vehicles, focusing on off-road settings. Evaluated the adaptability of deep learning architectures, particularly Vision Transformers (ViT), for semantic segmentation. Findings indicate that ViT architectures offer strong segmentation accuracy, with lower inference speed and memory footprint compared to traditional CNN architectures, highlighting their potential for improving off-road perception in autonomous vehicles.

At the same time, we presented our research group's implementation and comparisons of multiple ML architectures for image segmentation, developed during the Summer 2024 Machine Learning and Big Data (MLBD) Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU). The project focuses on segmenting various types of off-road terrain in images, to then be implemented in AI powered cars for autonomous traversability, utilizing deep learning techniques to improve accuracy and performance. We presented this at the summer REU poster presentation at Watt Family Innovation Center.
